About

I'm a computational neuroscientist based in Amsterdam, with degrees in both Artificial Intelligence and Brain & Cognitive Sciences.

My work centres on mechanistic models of neural computation — from biophysical spiking networks and rate models to deep learning architectures — to understand how the brain implements perception, cognition, and behaviour.

I'm especially interested in the interplay between cortical dynamics, predictive coding, and biologically plausible deep learning, and in bridging theoretical neuroscience with experimental data.

Research

2024  ·  PUBLISHED
Parallel Cortical & Subcortical Processing in Shallow Brain Networks ↗
A computational implementation of the shallow brain hypothesis — a CNN with continuous time-integration via neural ODEs in PyTorch, combined with a biologically plausible predictive coding + Wong–Wang architecture fitted against monkey reaction-time data. Published in peer-reviewed journal with equal-contribution authorship.

Cortical Disinhibition: Rate to Spiking Dynamics ↗
