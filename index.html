About

I'm a computational neuroscientist based in Amsterdam, with degrees in both Artificial Intelligence and Brain & Cognitive Sciences.

My work centres on mechanistic models of neural computation — from biophysical spiking networks and rate models to deep learning architectures — to understand how the brain implements perception, cognition, and behaviour.

I'm especially interested in the interplay between cortical dynamics, predictive coding, and biologically plausible deep learning, and in bridging theoretical neuroscience with experimental data.

Research

2024  ·  PUBLISHED
Parallel Cortical & Subcortical Processing in Shallow Brain Networks ↗
A computational implementation of the shallow brain hypothesis — a CNN with continuous time-integration via neural ODEs in PyTorch, combined with a biologically plausible predictive coding + Wong–Wang architecture fitted against monkey reaction-time data. Published in peer-reviewed journal with equal-contribution authorship.
Neural ODEs Predictive Coding Wong-Wang Model CNNs PyTorch TensorFlow SciPy
2026  ·  ONGOING
Cortical Disinhibition: From Rate to Spiking Dynamics ↗
Modelling excitatory pyramidal cells alongside inhibitory PV, SOM, and VIP interneuron populations. Analysing fixed-point stability of Wilson-Cowan equations via Jacobian eigenvalues and spectral radius; extending toward spiking dynamics with Leaky Integrate-and-Fire neurons.
Wilson-Cowan LIF Neurons Stability Analysis Interneurons SciPy
2025
Time-Frequency Analysis of EEG Across Psychopathologies
Literature review on cognitive control and performance monitoring. Led EEG data collection for a pilot study and conducted a full preprocessing pipeline — including TF-PCA — followed by multivariate regression and moderation analyses in R.
EEG Time-Frequency TF-PCA EEGLAB R
2023
Decentralised Locomotion via Central Pattern Generators ↗
Morphology-agnostic kinetic controller inspired by cephalopod neurophysiology, using decentralised CPGs in EvoTorch. Deployed on simulated and physical robots of varying body plans — BSc Honours thesis.
CPGs EvoTorch Bio-inspired Robotics Evolutionary Computation

Agent-Based Simulations of Collective Behaviour ↗
A collection of agent-based models describing collective and emergent behaviours across different domains — from flocking and synchronisation to ecosystem dynamics. Side explorations beyond the main research track.
